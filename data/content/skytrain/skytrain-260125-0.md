## SkyTrain

SkyTrain will continue to serve as the rapid transit backbone. It's honestly incredible that we have an automated metro, and we should seek to nurture and expand it.

A key upgrade, that would help with safety and capacity \(in the throughput and platform capacity senses\) are platform screen doors \(PSD\). They should be installed as soon as possible \(possible being whenever train fleets can finally have the same door positions, or if we decide to get those Japanese PSDs that shift to match door positions\). Obviously, it is a good idea to prevent people and intrusions in general from getting on the track, for safety and to ensure there's nothing that could cause problems for the train. But there's also capacity benefits for the system, as the PSDs enable more of the platform to be stood on by passengers, as there is no longer a fear of falling onto the tracks, and because PSDs allow people to easily predict where doors will stop at, allowing for faster boarding and lower dwell times. 

Train models, or rolling stock significantly affects performance. This plan assumes the following regarding rolling stock: there will be two different types of rolling stock, similar to today. The SkyTrain Mark V model, currently replacing the Mark I, and likely the Mark II soon, will serve as the main train for the SkyTrain fleet, while the Canada , South-Central, and maybe the new lines in Surrey will use 3m wide conventional EMU trains.
