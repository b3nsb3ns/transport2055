## Fraser Valley Express

This service is among the most important rail projects to be undertaken. This regional rail line aims to connect Vancouver, Abbotsford, and anything in between. This line should enable a level of connectivity never before seen in the region, and allow us to cope with several issues, including congestion, overcrowding on the Expo line, creating employment nodes that work, and housing. 

### Capacity

In order to avoid an overcrowded Expo line and transportation system at large, this line allows a new avenue to absorb that demand, especially those who are going further distances. 

### Housing

As people move or consider moving into the Fraser valley to reduce costs of living, they sacrifice connectivity to the rest of the Lower Mainland. This line makes this move less of a sacrifice. 

### Pacific Central Station

The choice of terminating at Pacific Central Station was mainly to avoid expensive tunneling in the other option, Waterfront Station. While both stations are good options, the drive to reduce cost in this plan favours Pacific Central. 

### Sharing Tracks

The tracks for this line should be built such that they are shared with other services, namely [West Coast Express](wce) and [Amtrak Cascades](cascades).
