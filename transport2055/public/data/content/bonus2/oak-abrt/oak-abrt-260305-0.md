## Oak Street Autonomous Bus Rapid Transit

This is an idea that I have experimented with, partly to solve Canada line capacity issues, and to combine two concepts that have a degree of synergy. 

### BRT Operating Costs

An autonomous bus solves an issue with BRT, which is high operating costs. Automation would allow the number of operators could be eliminated, or reduced \(as in, the 'driver' now serves as a 'captain', with control over a platoon of several vehicles\). 

### Autonomous Operations

With BRT features such as dedicated lanes and signal priority, this system takes it further with communication from other fixed infrastructure, such as traffic cameras, working with onboard and other fixed infrastructure \(i.e. traffic signals\) to control and run these buses safely. It is designed to avoid or limit sources of friction that autonomous vehicles normally deal with, such as other vehicles and pedestrians.
