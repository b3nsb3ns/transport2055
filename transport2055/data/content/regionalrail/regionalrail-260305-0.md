# Regional Rail

Regional rail is crucial, as people live further out in the Fraser Valley. Thus, there should be a high capacity and truly fast way to traverse the Fraser Valley, and compress the region such that those who live further out are not disadvantaged, creating a highly connected economic zone, and allow people to save money that otherwise would have gone to car ownership. 

Much of my regional rail plan, and even some plans for other transit layers, are derived from the [Mountain Valley Institute's excellent proposal for regional rail](https://www.mvx.vision/). I highly encourage you to read it, and I applaud their bold \(but not brash!\) designs. My plan differs in that it focuses on what we ought to be building in the near term \(however you may interpret 'near'\), avoiding friction, and using some alignments as other forms of transit [\(ex. South Central SkyTrain\)](s-central). 

In short, this an electrified, all-day, frequent regional rail network that aims to tackle travel time, regional equity, and capacity on the rest of the network.
