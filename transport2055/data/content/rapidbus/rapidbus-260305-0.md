# RapidBus

RapidBus will continue to be an important layer of the transit network, offering rapid transit services, without needing to commit to much additional infrastructure. The added speed, bus lanes, and branding create a service that enables the humble bus to be seen as a convenient form of transit that complements rail, and perhaps, precede rail as well. 

I see RapidBus as an essential layer in a future transit network, with many routes interspersed across Metro Vancouver, allowing TransLink to provide rapid transit at \(relatively\) low capital costs. 

## Folding Into BRT

RapidBus' identity as a fast urban bus service is likely to be diminished as 'regular' bus routes are likely to gain many of the priority measures \(and more\) that enable RapidBus to be fast, such as dedicated lanes, wide stop spacing, and signal priority. Not only that, there is also the introduction of express layers to busy bus routes, such as the 49X and the 20X. With this in mind, the measures that make RapidBus what it is becomes the baseline, minus the branding.  

There is also the introduction of [Bus Rapid Transit](brt), which aims to bring in all the stops; dedicated lanes on the majority of a route, signal priority, proper stations rather than simple stops, digital departure screens, etc. I suggest that, given the similary between RapidBus and BRT, and that many RapidBus routes should receive upgrades in a similar vein to TransLink's currrent BRT plans.

With the uplifting of regular bus service and the introduction of BRT in mind, it seems prudent to fold RapidBus and BRT together. The baseline for regular bus service should become similar to what RapidBus is today, so differentiation for RapidBus should be pursued. Similarly, the introduction of BRT allows the lessons learnt and infrastructure scaling to also be applied to RapidBus. With this in mind, they \(RapidBus and BRT\) could be combined into a coherent BRT service, allowing transit users to know on instinct what the function of RapidBus/BRT is, and shift expectations upwards in the context of bus infrastructure.
